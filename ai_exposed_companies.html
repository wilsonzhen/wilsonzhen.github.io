AI Cohort Exposure: Geographic vs Demographic Concentration (v2)

Each point is a ticker (averaged across brands). Click any point to expand its brands on the chart. Scored by geometric mean.

Methodology

  1. AI-exposed cohort definition. Based on Anthropic's labor market research identifying workers most affected by AI: higher-income (47% higher wages than average) and younger professionals (22-25 age group saw ~14% hiring decline). We proxy this with three dimensions: Income $100K+ (household income above $100K), Age 18-34, and Tech Hub (6 CBSAs: San Jose, San Francisco, Seattle, New York, Washington DC, Boston).
  2. Data sources. Demographic shares (Income, Age) are from Consumer Edge (CER) credit/debit card panel data, using the PERIOD_BRAND_DEMO table filtered to combined-usa1-usa2 methodology and calendar quarters 2025Q1-Q4. Tech Hub geographic share is from Earnest credit/debit card panel data, aggregating sales across the 6 tech hub CBSAs for 2025.
  3. Share calculation. For each dimension, the share equals AI-exposed spend divided by total spend (e.g., Income Share = spend by $100K+ households / total spend across all income brackets). This is a simple proportion of total.
  4. Exposure score (geometric mean). The composite score is the geometric mean of available raw shares (cube root of product for 3 dimensions, square root for 2). Unlike additive z-scores, this treats dimensions as complements: a company must score well on ALL dimensions to rank highly. A near-zero share on any dimension collapses the score, correctly reflecting that e.g. a company with 0% tech hub presence is not exposed to tech hub layoffs regardless of its demographic profile.
  5. Brand-level analysis. CER and Earnest report at brand level (e.g., WSM breaks into West Elm, Pottery Barn, Williams-Sonoma). Brand names are mapped between the two sources. Click any point to see the brand-level breakdown. Ticker-level scores are the mean of brand-level shares.
  6. Axes. X-axis shows Tech Hub Sales Share (Earnest). Y-axis shows the average of Income Share and Age Share (CER). Companies in the top-right have the highest concentration of AI-exposed customers on both geographic and demographic dimensions.
  7. Limitations. This measures static customer composition, not spending trend changes. Card panel data may not perfectly represent all consumer segments. Some CER brands lack a matched Earnest brand and use ticker-level tech hub share as fallback. Companies without any Earnest coverage lack the Tech Hub dimension and are excluded from this scatter view.
